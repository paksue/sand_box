  • Good first base near Munich center.
  • Use this stay for old town + main city day.
  • Ask front desk for Eiswürfel if you need ice.

Romantik Hotel Markusturm

Tue Jul 21 → Wed Jul 22
1 night · Rothenburg Old Town · check-in 3:00 PM or later · checkout 11:00 AM or earlier.

  • Best reason to overnight: Rothenburg after day-trippers leave.
  • Night Watchman Tour fits perfectly at 8 PM.
  • Historic hotel feel; do not expect U.S.-style ice machine.

Cocoon Hauptbahnhof

Wed Jul 22 → Sat Jul 25
3 nights · Ludwigsvorstadt-Isarvorstadt · check-in 3:00 PM or later · checkout 11:00 AM or earlier.

  • Best base for Dachau, airport, and Neuschwanstein train/rental car logistics.
  • Near Hauptbahnhof for food and late shopping.
  • Keep final morning simple.
Day 1 · Munich first look
Sunday · July 19

Arrival + Old Town Glow

First day should feel like landing inside the trip, not “checking off attractions.” Stay outdoors, eat simply, and let everyone adjust to the 6-hour time shift.

Arrival window

Airport → hotel → check in at Hotel Andra after 2 PM. Keep passports and hotel confirmation easy to access.

Late afternoon

Marienplatz, Neues Rathaus, Frauenkirche exterior, and a slow walk toward Viktualienmarkt.

Simple dinner

Pick a low-friction dinner near old town or hotel. Do not schedule a paid tour this evening.

Jet lag rule

Stay awake until at least 9 PM local time if possible. No long naps.

Photo ideaFamily photo in front of Neues Rathaus.
Teen rulePhone away in crowds and train stations.
Snack planBuy water/snacks near hotel before shops close.
Day 2 · Royal Munich
Monday · July 20

Munich City Day

The best full Munich day: one “grand interior” in the morning, one flexible choice in the afternoon, then a relaxed dinner.

Morning

Munich Residenz. The Antiquarium is the strongest “wow” interior and makes the day feel special.

Lunch

Viktualienmarkt / old town area. Good for grazing rather than one long sit-down meal.

Afternoon choice A

Deutsches Museum if the girls want science, technology, engineering, aviation, or hands-on exhibits.

Afternoon choice B

Nymphenburg Palace if the family wants royal gardens, palace photos, and a less dense museum experience.

Evening

Easy walk, early dinner, pack a small Rothenburg overnight bag.

Day 3 · Rothenburg overnight
Tuesday · July 21

Rothenburg: Walls, Towers, Night Watchman

This is the “story-book medieval town” day. The overnight makes it much better because the town changes after the day-trippers leave.

Morning travel

Leave Munich after breakfast. If using Bayern Ticket on a weekday, plan after 9 AM. Train is simplest; driving is best only if you already have a rental car.

Arrive + lunch

Drop bags or check in at Romantik Hotel Markusturm after 3 PM. Eat in the old town.

Afternoon route

Marktplatz → Plönlein → city wall walk → small shops. Keep this atmospheric, not rushed.

Dinner

Early enough to be ready for the 8 PM tour.

8 PM

Night Watchman Tour. This is the anchor activity for the overnight.

Must-doNight Watchman Tour.
Best photoPlönlein before/after crowds.
Do not overpackOne overnight bag is enough.
Day 4 · Medieval morning
Wednesday · July 22

Crime Museum + Return to Munich

Use the early morning for quiet Rothenburg, then one strong museum before returning to Munich.

Early morning

Walk the walls and lanes before day-trippers arrive. This is when Rothenburg feels most magical.

After breakfast

Medieval Crime and Justice Museum. Best “dark history” stop for teenagers because it connects law, punishment, fear, shame, authority, and society.

Optional add-on

Rothenburg Dungeon / Historiengewölbe if you still have energy. Treat it as a short bonus, not the main event.

Afternoon

Train or drive back to Munich. Check into Cocoon Hauptbahnhof after 3 PM.

Evening

Light dinner near Hauptbahnhof. Laundry/organize if needed. Confirm Dachau plan for tomorrow.

Day 5 · Serious history
Thursday · July 23

Dachau Morning + Gentle Munich

Worth doing with 10th graders, but do not stack it with another heavy site. Let the afternoon breathe.

Morning

S-Bahn/bus to Dachau Memorial. Target the official English tour if timing works.

Midday

Return to Munich. Eat something simple. Give everyone quiet time.

Afternoon

English Garden, Eisbach surfers, or a café. Keep this deliberately light.

Evening

Prepare for Swan Castle day: ticket screenshot, route, snacks, water, phone charging, comfortable shoes.

Day 6 · Swan Castle ticket 5:20 PM
Friday · July 24

Neuschwanstein: The Big Castle Day

Your fixed castle ticket is late afternoon, so this becomes a scenic full day around Hohenschwangau instead of a quick there-and-back.

Morning departure

Leave Munich around 9:30–10:30 AM depending on train/car plan. Bring snacks and a battery pack.

Midday

Arrive Hohenschwangau/Füssen area, lunch, lake walk by Alpsee, Museum of the Bavarian Kings if you want a cool indoor stop.

3:20–3:50 PM

Be in the village 1.5–2 hours before your Neuschwanstein admission time. Do not cut this close.

Before tour

Marienbrücke viewpoint if open and conditions are safe. Build in time for uphill walk/shuttle delays.

5:20 PM

Neuschwanstein Castle tour for 4. This is the fixed anchor of the entire trip.

Evening

Return to Munich late. Keep dinner flexible: train-station food, simple restaurant, or snacks.

Day 7 · Homeward buffer
Saturday · July 25

Checkout + Fly Home

Do not create a busy final morning. The win is leaving Munich calm, fed, and with documents organized.

Morning

Breakfast, pack, check room, gather passports, chargers, adapters, tickets, medicines, and souvenirs.

Checkout

Cocoon Hauptbahnhof checkout by 11 AM or earlier.

Airport transfer

Use S-Bahn/airport train route or taxi depending on luggage and flight timing. Build in international-flight buffer.

Return jet lag

After landing in Boston, expect early waking for 1–2 days. Stay awake until normal Boston bedtime if possible.

Final checkPassports, phones, wallets, glasses, meds.
Teen checkChargers and earbuds out of hotel outlets.
MoneySpend coins before leaving Europe.

Field notes

Small practical things that matter during the actual trip.

Transport

Install DB Navigator and MVV/MVGO before leaving Boston.
For regional train days, check Bayern Ticket first.
Munich transit is not “tap-and-go” like Boston. Buy a valid ticket before boarding.
Screenshot tickets because train stations can have weak signal.

Money

Bring two Visa/Mastercards and one ATM/debit card.
Withdraw about €200 after arrival; small places and bathrooms may need cash/coins.
If card machine asks USD or EUR, choose EUR.
Ask: “Kartenzahlung möglich?” = “Is card payment possible?”

Phones

Activate eSIM/roaming on arrival; switch cellular data to travel eSIM if using one.
Turn off data roaming on the home line if avoiding charges.
Do not hold phones loosely in train stations, crowds, or near curbs.
Each person carries a charged phone or battery pack on Swan Castle day.

Hotels + comfort

Hotels.com app installed and all bookings visible under Trips.
Screenshot each hotel confirmation and save addresses offline.
Do not expect U.S.-style ice makers. Ask: “Haben Sie Eiswürfel?”
Buy snacks/water near Hotel Andra and Cocoon; Rothenburg old-town shops may close earlier.
